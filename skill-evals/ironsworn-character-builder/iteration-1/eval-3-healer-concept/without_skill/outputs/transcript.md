# Character Creation Transcript — Healer Concept (Baseline / No Skill)

---

**Player:** I want to make a character. I know I want to be some kind of healer or herbalist type — bad at fighting, good at helping people. I'm not sure about the vows yet, can we figure those out together?

**GM:** That sounds like a wonderful character concept — healers are rare and precious in the Ironlands. Let's build this out together.

First, a name. The Ironlands tend toward short, grim-sounding names — Maren, Edda, Tor, Wren, Silje. Do any of those resonate, or do you have something in mind?

---

**Player:** I like Wren. That feels right — small, quick, practical.

**GM:** Wren it is. Good choice.

Now let's talk stats. In Ironsworn you have five stats rated 1–3, and you distribute them from the spread: one at 3, one at 2, two at 1, and one gets nothing (or you use the standard array: 3, 2, 2, 1, 1 — pick the arrangement that fits).

The five stats are:

- **Edge** — quickness, precision, ranged attacks
- **Heart** — courage, persuasion, bonds, morale
- **Iron** — physical power, direct combat
- **Shadow** — stealth, deception, cunning
- **Wits** — perception, knowledge, survival, healing

For a healer who's bad at fighting but good at helping people, I'd suggest:

- **Wits 3** — your herbalism, diagnosis, and practical knowledge
- **Heart 2** — your warmth and ability to comfort the frightened
- **Edge 1** — you can be quick when you need to
- **Shadow 1** — a little situational awareness
- **Iron 1** — you can swing something if you must, but you'd rather not

Does that spread feel right, or would you adjust anything?

---

**Player:** That feels exactly right. Wits 3, Heart 2, the rest at 1.

**GM:** Perfect. Wren is sharp, warm, and not built for a brawl.

Now, assets. You pick three to start. For a healer/herbalist, here are some strong fits:

**Healer** *(Path asset)* — This is the obvious core. It gives you bonuses when you Heal others and lets you create medicine and salves from supplies. As you upgrade it, you can heal debilities and eventually treat the dying. This should almost certainly be one of your three.

**Herbalist** — Wait, in Ironsworn base, Healer *is* the herbalist/medic asset. It covers everything from field medicine to crafting remedies. You'll want this.

**Empath** *(Path asset)* — Lets you read people's emotions and ease stress. When you Forge a Bond or Aid your Ally, you get an edge. This fits someone whose strength is connection and support.

**Bonded** *(Path asset)* — Deepens your existing bonds — you start with more of them and they give you extra momentum when called upon. Good for a character whose community matters.

**Seer** *(Path asset)* — Premonitions and visions. Maybe a little mystical for a purely practical herbalist, but interesting if you want a spiritual dimension.

**Companion options** — A **Dog** or a **Horse** is always useful. If you imagine Wren traveling between villages, a horse makes sense. If you imagine her with a faithful hound who helps sniff out medicinal plants, a dog could be lovely.

I'd suggest: **Healer + Empath + Bonded** as a core "village healer who knows everyone and feels what they feel" build. But what calls to you?

---

**Player:** Healer and Empath feel essential. For the third... Bonded feels right. She's tied to her community.

**GM:** Excellent. Healer, Empath, Bonded — Wren is rooted, perceptive, and skilled.

Now for vows. You said you weren't sure yet — let's figure it out. Vows are the heart of Ironsworn, the promises that drive you forward. Characters typically start with one or two.

Let me ask a few questions to help find them naturally:

**1. Why does Wren heal?** Is it duty, a gift she was born with, penance for something, love of a community?

**2. Is there someone she's already failed?** A patient she couldn't save, a village struck by plague when she was elsewhere?

**3. What threatens the people she cares for?** Plague, raiders, famine, a supernatural blight on the land?

Take your time — any of those spark something?

---

**Player:** I think she heals because she loves her community, her village. And yes — there was a sickness last winter. She managed to save most people but lost a few, including an older woman who had taught her herbalism. She feels like she failed her. Maybe she vowed to find a cure or a better treatment so it can't happen again? And maybe her community is still struggling and she wants to make sure they survive the next winter?

**GM:** That's beautiful, and it gives you two vows that feel completely authentic to who Wren is.

**Vow 1 — Formidable: "I will find a cure for the winter sickness that took Maren."**

This is a long, difficult quest — tracking down rare ingredients, seeking out other healers or shamans in distant settlements, maybe even venturing to ruins or the deep wilderness where the plants grow that can treat this illness. Formidable feels right: it's not impossible, but it will test her.

**Vow 2 — Troublesome: "I will see my village through the coming winter."**

This is immediate, practical, and personal. It drives her to gather supplies, trade for medicines, tend to the sick, strengthen bonds with her neighbors. It'll be fulfilled within the story's near term, which is satisfying. Troublesome means progress comes in smaller increments, which fits the nature of community care.

Both vows connect directly to who she is, why she travels, and why she can't stay still.

Do those feel right? Or would you adjust the names, scope, or rank?

---

**Player:** Those feel perfect. "Maren" — that's the woman who taught her. I love that.

**GM:** It's a good name for a teacher and a loss. Let's make her matter.

Here's Wren's complete starting character:

---

**Name:** Wren
**Stats:** Wits 3 | Heart 2 | Edge 1 | Shadow 1 | Iron 1
**Assets:** Healer, Empath, Bonded
**Health:** 5 | **Spirit:** 5 | **Supply:** 5 | **Momentum:** +2

**Vows:**
- *Find a cure for the winter sickness that took Maren* — Formidable (0/10 progress)
- *See my village through the coming winter* — Troublesome (0/10 progress)

**Bonds:** Your village (we'll name it when you're ready), the memories of Maren.

---

One last thing: where does the story begin? Is Wren leaving the village for the first time in search of a cure, or does it start with a new crisis arriving on her doorstep?

---

**Player:** She's about to leave. There's a traveling trader who claims to have heard of a healer in the eastern foothills who might know something about the illness. She's going to follow that lead.

**GM:** Then we begin. Wren shoulders her satchel of remedies, says goodbye to the people who depend on her, and walks east toward a name she was given by a stranger.

The Ironlands are cold, and she has promises to keep.
